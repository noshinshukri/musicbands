AC/DC

AC/DC is an Australian rock band formed in Sydney in 1973 by brothers Malcolm and Angus Young. Known for their high-energy performances and simple, powerful riffs, they are one of the most successful bands in rock history.

Genre: Hard rock, heavy metal
Members: Angus Young (lead guitar), Brian Johnson (vocals), Stevie Young (rhythm guitar), Cliff Williams (bass), Phil Rudd (drums)
Famous Albums: Highway to Hell (1979), Back in Black (1980), The Razors Edge (1990), Ballbreaker (1995)
